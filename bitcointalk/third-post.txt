[b]1. Abstract[/b]
The Iceberg Protocol introduces a modular, high-integrity framework for decentralized organizations. By leveraging the [b]Stellar Soroban[/b] smart contract engine, we provide a self-regulating MLM (Multi-Level Marketing) structural layer decoupled from business logic via a proprietary [b]Message-Bus (Queue) Architecture[/b]. Our core protocol manages the issuance and stability of the [b]CYBERFORINT (CYF)[/b], a dynamically-backed digital asset, while providing an open-access utility for third-party developers to launch independent hierarchical ventures using the ZSOZSO utility token.

[b]2. The "Iceberg" Structural Philosophy[/b]
The protocol distinguishes between the [b]Immutable Logic[/b] (Submerged) and the [b]Dynamic Interface[/b] (Surface).

[list]
[*] [b]Submerged Layer:[/b] A recursive, N-level deep MLM tree. Each node is a standalone smart contract acting as a gateway.
[*] [b]Surface Layer:[/b] One-page JavaScript/HTML applications or simple mobile App's ("The surface of Iceberg Summits, Roots") where users, Pinguins, interact with financial instruments (Minting/Burning) without needing to understand the underlying complex hierarchy.
[/list]

[b]3. Decentralized Hierarchy & Evolution[/b]
Unlike traditional centralized MLM systems, the Iceberg Protocol utilizes a [b]Non-Custodial Tree Model[/b].

[b]3.1 Recursive Deployment (The Sponsor Chain)[/b]
Every node is deployed by its "Sponsor" contract. During initialization, the upstream data of 6 ancestral levels is passed down, ensuring that rewards and data packets always have a verified path to the Root. This creates a chain of trust established at the moment of the [b]WASM deployment[/b].

[b]3.2 Heartbeat & The Pruning Protocol[/b]
System hygiene is maintained through "Proof of Presence." Users must interact with their node to maintain its [i]Active[/i] status.

[list]
[*] [b]Notification Period:[/b] If a sponsor becomes inactive, a child node issues a "Wake-up" call via the on-chain messaging system.
[*] [b]The Resolution Choice:[/b] After a 168-hour grace period, the child can choose to [b]Support[/b] (pay the sponsor's storage rent to maintain hierarchy) or [b]Prune[/b] (sever the link).
[*] [b]Promotion to Root:[/b] Pruning results in the child node becoming a new "Root Node." It gains full governance over its own branch, including the right to set PID parameters and custom fee structures - a new Iceberg Summit.
[*] [b]About inactive leafs:[/b] When a leaf becomes inactive, the process is similar, except that the sponsor is the awakener or absorber.
[*] [b]Incentivized Activity:[/b] Active participation is rewarded, while dormant nodes risk losing their resources and hierarchical benefits.
[*] [b]Decentralized Evolution:[/b] This mechanism allows the MLM tree to evolve organically, with active participants rising to leadership roles, ensuring a dynamic and self-sustaining ecosystem.
[/list]

[b]4. Technical Engine: Message-Bus & Ports[/b]
To achieve high scalability and modularity, we implement a [b]Port-based Messaging System[/b]. Contracts do not call each other's functions directly in a rigid way; instead, they send standardized "Data Packets" to a [b]Queue[/b].

This architecture allows a "Plug-and-Play" approach:

[list]
[*] [b]Vault Ports:[/b] For managing asset baskets and collateral.
[*] [b]RWA Ports:[/b] For late-stage integration of audited physical assets.
[*] [b]Third-Party Ports:[/b] External projects can hook into an Iceberg MLM tree by simply implementing the [code]MessagePort[/code] interface.
[/list]

[b]5. Algorithmic Market Dynamics & The CYBERFORINT (CYF)[/b]
The [b]CYBERFORINT (CYF)[/b] asset is the native, dynamically-backed crypto-asset of the Iceberg ecosystem. The total supply of CYF is zero at launch; assets are introduced into circulation [b]solely through the Minting process[/b] initiated by active Root Nodes and Pingvin users.

[b]CYF Issuance & Stability:[/b] CYF is not pegged to any existing fiat currency. Its value is stabilized algorithmically by a digital [b]PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) Controller[/b] implemented in fixed-point Rust math. This controller monitors the network's Mint/Burn velocity and adjusts the collateralization ratio in real-time, ensuring unique market dynamics based purely on internal ecosystem activity.

[b]Resonance & Noise Filtering:[/b] Root nodes are responsible for setting the [i]Alpha Filter[/i] for their respective branches. This parameter allows the Root to manage how aggressively their local market reacts to volatility, enabling a healthy competition for user stability.
